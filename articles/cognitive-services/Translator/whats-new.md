# What's new in Translator

Learn what's new in the Translator service. Bookmark this page to stay up to date with release notes, feature enhancements, and documentation updates.

## February 2021

**New Release**:  [Document Translation](document-translation/overview.md) is available as a preview feature of the Translator Service. Preview features are still in development and aren't meant for production use. They're made available on a "preview" basis so customers can get early access and provide feedback. The Document Translation feature enables you to translate large documents and process batch documents in various file formats while still preserving the original structure and format.

Translator service now has [text translation language support](language-support.md#text-translation) for the following languages:

* Albanian, an Indo-European language unrelated to any other modern language and spoken by nearly 8 million.
* Amharic, an official language of Ethiopia spoken by approximately 32 million people. It's also the liturgical language of the Ethiopian Orthodox church.
* Armenian, the official language of Armenia with 5-7 million speakers.
* Azerbaijani, a Turkic language spoken by approximately 23 million people.
* Khmer, the official language of Cambodia with approximately 16 million speakers.
* Myanmar, the official language of Myanmar, spoken as a first language by approximately 33 million.
* Nepali, the official language of Nepal with approximately 16 million native speakers.
* Tigrinya, a language spoken in Eritrea and northern Ethiopia with nearly 11 million speakers.

## January 2021

Translator service now has [text translation language support](language-support.md#text-translation) for Inuktitut, one of the principal Inuit languages of Canada. Inuktitut is one of eight official aboriginal languages in the Northwest Territories.

The V2 platform enables you to build custom models with all document types (training, testing, tuning, phrase dictionary, and sentence dictionary). ## October 2020

Translator service now has [text translation language support](language-support.md#text-translation) for **Canadian French**. Although Canadian French and European French are similar to one another and are mutually understandable, there can be significant differences in vocabulary, grammar, writing, and pronunciation. Over 7 million Canadians (20 percent of the population) speak French as their first language.

## September 2020

Translator service now has [text translation language support](language-support.md#text-translation) for **Assamese** also knows as **Axomiya**.  Assamese / Axomiya is primarily spoken in Eastern India by approximately 14 million people. *See* [Language Support for text Translation](language-support.md#text-translation)

## August 2020

**New Release**: [Virtual network](/azure/cognitive-services/cognitive-services-virtual-networks) capabilities and [Azure private links](/azure/private-link/private-link-overview) for Translator are now available to the general public (GA).  Azure private links allow you to access Translator and your Azure hosted services over a private endpoint in your virtual network. You can use private endpoints for Translator to allow clients on a virtual network to securely access data over a private link.

**New Release**: [Custom Translator V2 phase 1](https://azure.microsoft.com/updates/ctaug2020/) is now available. The newest version of Custom Translator will roll out in two phases to provide quicker translation and quality improvements, and allow you to keep your training data in the region of your choice.

* Translator service now has [text translation language support](language-support.md#text-translation)
for the following languages:

  * **Northern (Kurmanji) Kurdish** and **Central (Sorani) Kurdish**. Most Kurdish texts are written in Kurmanji and Sorani.

  * **Dari** and **Pashto**, the two official languages of Afghanistan.

  * **Odia** is a classical language spoken by 35 million people in India and across the world. It joins Bangla, Gujarati, Hindi, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Punjabi, Tamil, Telugu, Urdu, and English as the twelfth most used language of India supported by Microsoft Translator.
