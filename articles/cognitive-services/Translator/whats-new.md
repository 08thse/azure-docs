# What's new in Form Recognizer

Learn what's new in the Translator service. Bookmark this page to stay up-to-date with release notes, feature enhancements, and documentation updates.

## November 2020

Custom Translator V2 upgrade (full trainability) is generally available (https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/updates/ctnov2020/)

## October 2020

Translator service now has [text translation language support]((language-support.md#text-translation) for **Canadian French**. Although Canadian French and European French are similar to one another and are mutually understandable, there can be significant differences in vocabulary, grammar, writing, and pronunciation. Over seven million Canadians (20 percent of the population) speak French as their first language.

## September 2020

Translator service now has [text translation language support]((language-support.md#text-translation) for **Assamese** also knows as **Axomiya**.  Assamese / Axomiya is primarily spoken in Eastern India by approximately 14 million people. *See* [Language Support for text Translation](language-support.md#text-translation)

## August 2020

* **Custom Translator v2** is now available. The newest version of Custom Translator boasts improved quality and allows you to keep your training data in the region of your choice.

* Translator service now has [text translation language support]((language-support.md#text-translation)
for the following:

  * **Northern (Kurmanji) Kurdish** and **Central (Sorani) Kurdish**. Most Kurdish texts are written in Kurmanji and Sorani.

  * **Dari** and **Pashto**, the two official languages of Afghanistan.

  * **Odia**, a classical language spoken by 35 million people in India and across the world. It joins Bangla, Gujarati, Hindi, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Punjabi, Tamil, Telugu, Urdu and English as the twelfth most used language of India supported by Microsoft Translator.
